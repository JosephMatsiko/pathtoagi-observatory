# Invitation: independent scoring of Frame Construction Suite runs

*Draft for the operator to send (also queued as a Gmail draft, 2026-07-02).

Dear —,

I operate **pathtoAGI — the Observatory** (https://pathtoagi-observatory.netlify.app),
a public epistemic instrument tracking whether frontier AI systems can
construct new scientific frames — not recall or interpolate them, construct
them — under contamination-disciplined conditions. It runs autonomously,
maintained by an adversarial mesh of three model families under a
hash-pinned constitution and a thirteen-gate verdict protocol; every claim,
forecast, revision, and mistake is public, and release commits are signed and
anchored via OpenTimestamps.

Four sealed synthetic worlds have now been run and graded — three-line
summary of what they found:

- Two disclosed/permissive worlds were solved completely (parameter
  identification / tool-assisted discovery — not frame construction).
- A hardened world with the frame withheld and code forbidden produced a
  **calibrated non-answer**: a model correctly caught itself falling for a
  misleading pattern and refused to guess, without finding the true rule.
- A wrong-frame-attractor world produced the record's **first evidence
  against** frame construction: a frontier model confidently constructed a
  plausible wrong frame, scored 0/44 on held-out prediction, and self-reported
  rejecting the trap while actually falling into it.

Every run is published in full — prompts, transcripts, sealed laws, reveals,
and grading — at https://pathtoagi-observatory.netlify.app/runs/

The named limitation that matters most: **every one of these was
self-graded.** Under the suite's own asymmetry, a self-graded result is an
upper bound (if it passed) or suggestive (if it failed) — never verdict-moving.
The record cannot strengthen its own claims; that is exactly why I am writing.

**The ask:** would you serve as an independent scorer for one or more of these
runs? Concretely:

- You receive the frozen, already-public artifacts — the same ones anyone can
  read.
- You grade against the pre-registered rubric, in whatever depth you judge
  right, and are welcome to attack the rubric itself.
- Your grading is published verbatim on the record, under your name, alongside
  the instrument's own — including everywhere you disagree with it.
- No compensation is offered, deliberately: a paid grader is a hired one. The
  time cost is roughly one to three hours per run.

If you'd rather try a probe yourself first, there is now a playable version —
including a version with no mathematics at all — at
https://pathtoagi-observatory.netlify.app/play/

The record's honest state, including its retracted errors and an ongoing
incident log, is at https://pathtoagi-observatory.netlify.app/log/ — I would
rather you judge the instrument by its mistakes than by this letter.
