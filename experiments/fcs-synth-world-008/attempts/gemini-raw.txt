### 1. Observational Decidability
**Can the observational samples alone decide which mechanism is true?**
**No.** 

**Reasoning:**
In the observational dataset, there are no active interventions. Both candidate causal mechanisms are observationally equivalent because they imply the exact same joint distribution over the observed variables $(a, b, c)$ modulo the prime $p = 103$. 
Specifically, the underlying system of equations fits the 24 observations perfectly with:
* $b \equiv 41a + 50 \pmod{103}$
* $c \equiv 24b + 77 \pmod{103}$ (under CHAIN)

Substituting the first equation into the second yields:
$$c \equiv 24(41a + 50) + 77 \equiv 57a + 41 \pmod{103}$$

Under the FORK mechanism, the true structural equations are:
* $b \equiv 41a + 50 \pmod{103}$
* $c \equiv 57a + 41 \pmod{103}$

Both mechanisms yield the identical deterministic mapping $(a, b, c)$ for any observational value of $a$. Without an intervention, it is impossible to distinguish if $c$ is determined via $b$ (CHAIN) or via $a$ (FORK).

### 2. Discriminating Intervention
To resolve the causal ambiguity, the single necessary intervention is to **externally force (intervene on) variable $b$ to a new value $do\_b$ and observe variable $c$**. 

* **Under the CHAIN mechanism ($a \to b \to c$):** Intervening on $b$ blocks the causal influence of $a$ on $b$, but the forced value of $b$ will propagate to $c$. Thus, $c$ will change according to $c \equiv 24(do\_b) + 77 \pmod{103}$.
* **Under the FORK mechanism ($b \leftarrow a \to c$):** $c$ is causally independent of $b$ given $a$. Intervening on $b$ breaks the link $a \to b$ but leaves the path $a \to c$ intact. Thus, $c$ remains unaffected by the intervention on $b$ and will continue to follow $c \equiv 57a + 41 \pmod{103}$.

* **Confidence Level:** **100%**

Furthermore, the predictions under CHAIN and FORK are internally consistent with matching data points from the observations (e.g., in Probe 8, the FORK prediction for $a=37$ matches the observed value $c=90$ in Sample 1, and the CHAIN prediction for $do\_b=57$ matches the observed value $c=3$ in Sample 20).
