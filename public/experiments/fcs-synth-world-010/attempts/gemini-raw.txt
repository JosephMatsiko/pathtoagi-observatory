### 1. Sufficiency of Observed Variables
**No**, the observed variables $(a, b, c, d)$ are not sufficient to explain the interventional data.

**Reason:** 
In the observational data, there is a strict linear relationship between $a$, $b$, and $c$. However, the interventional records show that forcing $a$ to a new value (`do(a=42)`) does not change $b$ or $c$, and forcing $b$ (`do(b=61)`) or $c$ (`do(c=21)`) does not affect the others. The only way to explain this correlation without direct causal links between $a$, $b$, and $c$ is by positing a **hidden/unobserved common cause** $u$ that determines $a$, $b$, and $c$.
