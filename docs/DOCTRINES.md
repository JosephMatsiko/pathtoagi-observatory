# Doctrines

Status: adopted digest from Omnibus v2.0. These doctrines are interpretive
guidance for building and adjudicating the Observatory; they do not override
the constitution, precedent register, or verdict protocol.

## Core Doctrines

1. One question, deeply. Frame construction remains the apex question; adjacent
   tracks support it rather than replacing it.
2. No coronations. The system may run autonomously; it may not crown itself.
3. Evidence before status. Every public status label must point to record
   objects, not institutional mood.
4. Convergence, not deletion. Omnibus adoption migrates the running system
   toward the specification while preserving anchored history.
5. Failures become objects. Errors are not merely apologized for; they become
   revisions, incidents, controls, and future tests.
6. Reproduction is a surface, not an afterthought. A claim that cannot be
   inspected, rerun, challenged, or scored remains provisional.
7. Interface is epistemology. Pages, APIs, dashboards, and command-line surfaces
   must make uncertainty, provenance, and challenge paths visible.

## External-Ecosystem Lessons

- Frontier labs demonstrate that deployment, governance, and evaluation cannot
  be separated once systems act in the world.
- Benchmark ecosystems demonstrate that a score is weaker than the protocol,
  contamination controls, and reproduction story around it.
- Assurance and incident practices demonstrate that durable trust comes from
  named objects, public corrections, and audit trails.
- Operational ontology systems demonstrate that governed objects need permitted
  actions: claims can be challenged, runs can be reproduced, incidents can be
  repaired, and precedents can be overturned only through named procedures.

## Implementation Bias

Prefer small, typed record objects over prose-only claims. Prefer public,
deterministic checks over private confidence. Prefer visible uncertainty over
clean theater. Prefer a slower verdict over a premature crown.
